The coffee shop owner

Sarah ran Three Cups Coffee entirely through Instagram DMs and a Google Business page.

Last month, her account got flagged for "suspicious activity" — locked for 5 days. No orders. No way to reach customers. The queue went silent.

She moved to an owned website this month.

Three Cups now has her own online ordering system, customer email list, and backup checkout. Instagram is just a feeder now, not storefront.

The fitness coach

Marcus built his client base on Twitter threads and a booking platform that took 15% of every session.

When the platform changed their payout structure, his monthly income dropped $800 — overnight.

He switched to a simple 3-page site with Stripe payments embedded directly.

Same booking flow. No middleman taking a cut. He keeps every dollar he earns.

The boutique

Elegance Floral used Square Online for 3 years. Custom templates were limited, their branding was squeezed into someone else's design language, and they couldn't add the client portal customers kept asking for.

They moved to a custom-built site last quarter.

Now the brand looks like their brand. The client portal exists. The checkout flow works exactly how they need it to work.

What These Have in Common

They stopped renting. They started owning.

The platforms didn't get deleted — they just got demoted. Instagram is now for discovery, not transactions. The booking platform got replaced with direct payments. Square Online got swapped for a site that actually matches their brand.

Control means:

  • You can't be locked out
  • You don't pay unnecessary platform fees
  • Your data belongs to you
  • Your brand looks like you
